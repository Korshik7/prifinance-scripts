💰 Taxes & Legality

"Is this 100% legal? 0% sounds too good to be true."
▼
100% legal. Territorial taxation is written into Hong Kong law — not a loophole, it's the system. Hong Kong is a legitimate international financial centre, not a blacklisted jurisdiction. Thousands of international trading companies use exactly this structure. The key is that your business genuinely operates internationally — goods never pass through HK.
"What if my goods pass through Hong Kong port?"
▼
If goods physically enter Hong Kong territory — that portion of income becomes taxable. Rate is 8.25% on the first HKD 2M of profit, 16.5% above that. For your model — direct China to Europe/Saudi — this is not relevant. The structure requires goods to ship directly, never touching HK.
"Will Saudi or UAE tax authorities have issues with this?"
▼
You're a UAE resident — UAE has 0% personal income tax, so nothing to declare there. Saudi Arabia taxes its own tax residents — you're a UAE resident, not a Saudi tax resident. The HK company is a separate legal entity that files its own returns in Hong Kong. The structure is clean. I'd always recommend double-checking with your local tax advisor for full peace of mind — but this is a standard setup.
"Is Hong Kong considered an offshore jurisdiction?"
▼
Not in the traditional sense. Hong Kong has its own tax system, annual reporting requirements, and mandatory audit. It's internationally respected and not on any blacklist. What makes it attractive is the territorial tax system — not secrecy. That's why banks and partners worldwide accept HK entities without issues.

🏢 Company Structure & Privacy

"My name won't appear anywhere? Are you sure?"
▼
With a nominee structure — correct. The nominee director and shareholder appear in the public registry, not you. The Trust Declaration is a private legal document confirming your beneficial ownership. The Power of Attorney gives you full operational control. This is a legal, widely-used privacy instrument — not concealment. Thousands of international business owners use it.
"What exactly is a company secretary and why is it mandatory?"
▼
It's a requirement under Hong Kong law — every registered company must have a local company secretary. They handle statutory filings — annual returns, compliance notifications, registry updates. They have no access to your bank accounts, no influence on business decisions. Think of them as an administrative compliance function. We provide this as part of the package.
"Can I add a partner or shareholder later?"
▼
Yes — adding a shareholder is a standard procedure, we handle the corporate structure update and issue the relevant documents. It's simpler to decide on ownership from the start, but it's not a problem to change later.
"Do I need a licence for my type of business?"
▼
For standard trading, supply chain, logistics — no licence needed. Licences are only required for financial services: banking, insurance, trust services, crypto funds. Construction materials trading is completely clean — register and go.

🏦 Banking

"Why can't I just open at a local HK bank like HSBC or DBS?"
▼
Local HK banks like DBS and Citibank have strict requirements — they want a local resident director in the company structure (not just a nominee), a detailed business plan, existing account history, and often a minimum deposit. New companies without a trading track record get rejected regularly. That's why we use Chouzhou Bank in China or the HK online bank for new setups — they work with new companies and our relationship means smooth processing.
"Will my Chinese suppliers accept payments from a Hong Kong company?"
▼
Absolutely — HK companies are extremely well-known and trusted in China. Chinese suppliers work with HK entities all the time. If you use Chouzhou Bank, you can pay directly in CNY, which suppliers often prefer and can get you better exchange rates.
"I can't travel to China — I don't have a visa."
▼
Two options. First — China offers a 140-hour visa-free transit policy. You fly in from a third country (not your country of departure), sign the bank documents, and fly out. Or use Singapore as a stopover — 96-hour transit, no visa needed. Our clients do this regularly — it's 1–2 days. Alternatively, the HK online bank is fully remote — no travel at all.
"What if the bank rejects my application?"
▼
We prepare the full application package before submission — business description, transaction flow documentation, KYC docs — specifically to maximise approval chances. If there's an issue on our end, we move to the next option. We know what triggers rejections and we avoid them upfront.
"Can I use my existing UAE personal account for the company?"
▼
You could temporarily, but UAE banks will start asking questions when they see large regular transactions from China going through a personal account — it raises compliance flags. A corporate account in Hong Kong keeps business and personal finances clean and separate. It's also what your suppliers and buyers will expect for a proper business relationship.
